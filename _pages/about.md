I am an Applied A.I. Scientist with industrial and academic experience developing machine learning algorithms and software for predictive analytics. My work sits at the intersection of speech science, digital health, and artificial intelligence — translating clinical research questions into scalable data pipelines and biomarker algorithms.

Currently I serve as **Director of Speech Sciences at [Redenlab](https://redenlab.com)** (0.8 FTE) and **Senior Post-doctoral Researcher at the University of Melbourne**, Department of Audiology and Speech Pathology (0.2 FTE).

## Research Interests

- Digital speech biomarkers for neurological disorders
- Automatic speech recognition (ASR) and speaker recognition
- Speech synthesis and voice deepfake detection
- Forensic phonetics and speaker verification
- Natural language processing and generative AI

## Background

I completed my **Ph.D. *summa cum laude*** in Computational Linguistics / Phonetics and Speech Sciences at the **University of Zurich** (2015–2019), funded by the Swiss National Science Foundation (SNSF) and a Forschungskredit Candoc Scholarship. Prior to that I held an ERASMUS MUNDUS fellowship, earning M.Sc. degrees from **KTH Royal Institute of Technology** (Stockholm) and **Universitat Politècnica de Catalunya** (Barcelona).

My industry experience spans clinical-trial speech technology (Redenlab), ASR research (Telepathy Labs, Zurich), COVID-19 vocal biomarker diagnostics (VoiceMed, Luxembourg), forensic speaker verification (CFPA), and enterprise IT (Tata Consultancy Services).

I have been an invited speaker at the IAAM Summit and appeared on Swiss national television (SRF Einstein) and radio to explain deepfake voice technology to the general public.
